# Editing And Risk Rules

Read this file completely before editing.

## Meaning-Preservation Gate

Reject or pause an edit that changes any of these without explicit researcher authority:

- affirmation, negation, comparison, condition, modality, or uncertainty;
- causal, associative, predictive, explanatory, or descriptive force;
- effect direction, magnitude, significance, precision, or support status;
- construct definition, variable role, theoretical mechanism, or hypothesis meaning;
- population, sample, condition, context, time, level, boundary, or generalization;
- confirmatory, exploratory, robustness, sensitivity, or post hoc status;
- contribution, implication, limitation, or recommendation scope;
- citation key, citation placement relative to claims, Study ID, table, figure, or hypothesis ID.

When a candidate edit crosses this gate, retain the original text and create an Author Query. Fluency never authorizes a scientific change.

## Edit Risk

### Low

Spelling, punctuation, capitalization, agreement, articles, prepositions, obvious usage, formatting, and local grammar with no plausible meaning change.

Apply directly and record material patterns rather than every mechanical correction unless the user requests a complete redline log.

### Medium

Sentence splitting or merging, clause movement, removal of repetition, pronoun replacement, local transition changes, voice changes, tense changes, nominalization changes, and compression that could affect emphasis or attachment.

Apply only after a source-to-edit check confirms the same actor, action, object, direction, condition, scope, evidential force, and citation attachment.

### High

Any edit involving hypotheses, definitions, causal or mediation language, moderation or boundary conditions, statistical results, null or contrary findings, contribution claims, novelty claims, limitations, policy or practical recommendations, population generalization, or removal of a scientific qualifier.

Do not apply without explicit authority. A researcher-approved exact correction may be applied and logged with its authority.

## Minimum-Sufficient Editing

- Prefer the smallest edit that resolves the language problem.
- Preserve necessary complexity and disciplinary terminology.
- Do not standardize every sentence into the same length, cadence, voice, or transition pattern.
- Remove redundancy only when no premise, qualifier, contrast, scope marker, or citation function is lost.
- Do not replace precise technical verbs with stronger causal verbs.
- Do not improve apparent coherence by inventing a missing premise.
- Do not add citations, evidence, examples, variables, mechanisms, findings, or contributions.
- Do not change a citation key or move a citation across a substantive claim boundary.

## Terminology

Apply a researcher-approved terminology register. Preserve legitimate distinctions between related constructs, outcomes, samples, conditions, analyses, and Studies.

When inconsistent terms may refer to the same construct, flag the locations and request a canonical decision. Do not normalize by frequency, recency, or rhetorical preference.

## Author Query Record

Use:

`Query ID | Location | Original text | Language problem | Scientific meaning risk | Why editing paused | Bounded options | Protected content | Required authority | Status`

Options may explain linguistic consequences but must not decide theory, design, evidence, or results.

## Verification

For every Medium-risk edit and every researcher-authorized High-risk edit, compare source and output for:

`actor | action | object | direction | condition | negation | modality | scope | time | evidence strength | numerical values | IDs | citation attachment`

Any unexplained difference is semantic drift. Restore the source or require an author decision.
